PROBLEM STATEMENT
 Hinge is a dating app whose mission statement is “designed to be deleted.” The app is known for having users who are searching for serious relationships.

It has great features to start a conversation, but not enough to keep it going. To foster meaningful connections, ‍a post-match "date" feature is proposed.

IDEATION • Affinity Mapping

By dividing interview and survey records into categories for comparison and observation, users' pain points took a larger portion than their positive or neutral experiences, hinting an overall sense of fatigue and boredom.

By scoping down to pain points, this mapping provides a clearer vision of what to improve and redesign in order to benefit users. The issue with disingenuous users is most frequently occurred among Tinder users. As this study focuses on Hinge, we pinned it down to chatting fatigue and unsuccessful matches or dates.

As Hinge is a relationship-focused app, users' ultimate goal is to meet their future partners. Chats that aren't moving forward as dates pose as a major roadblock. After a series of brainstorming and taking users' fatigue towards dating apps into account, the design question was reframed to "How can we make the post-match experience more engaging that helps users to set up a date?"
